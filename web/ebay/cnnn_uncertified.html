I consider this coin to be MS-64, because of the following details from the PCGS Grading Standards. I have tried to be conservative, and you may well judge that the coin should have a different grade.

I am not a coin dealer or grader. To try to grade coins, I use several professional publications. The best, in my opinion, is Official Guide to Coin Grading and Counterfeit Detection, published by the Professional Coin Grading Service (PCGS). In this book, the standards for grading Mint State coins are explained and illustrated in great detail. Graders at the PCGS examine hundreds of coins every day, of every denomination, series, and condition. Their collective wisdom has been published in the Official Guide. Here is an excerpt from the book:

"Morgan dollars were usually transported in bags; thus most have marks and other surface impairments. Finding any date, other than the most common ones, is grades of MS-67 and higher is nearly impossible. Light contact on the highest points is evident even in the highest grades, as those coins that were stored in rolls also received some contact.

"For the lower grades of MS-60 to MS-62, marks are plentiful, with the face usually bearing the brunt of the impairments. The large, smooth cheek of Miss Liberty is a magnet for marks, with the field also being a very common area of impairment. Album slide marks are also common, as this is one of the most popular and highly collected series of any denomination. The reverse has less field area and sometimes marks are hidden in the eagle's feathers. The breast area is a high point, and severe marks are often seen on this device."

Here are some of the grading criteria from the PCGS Guide.

MS-65

Marks: There may be some scattered marks, hairlines, or other minor defects. If the flaws are in a main focal area, they must be minor and few. Hidden marks and hairlines can be larger. On larger coins, there can be marks/hairlines in the fields and in the devices, but no major ones.
Strike: The coin will be well struck.
Luster: The luster will be average or above average, and any toning can only slightly impede the luster.
Eye Appeal: The eye appeal will be average or above. A coin may grade MS-65 with scattered, light marks, but with great luster and strike; or a coin with virtually no marks but a slightly impeded luster also could be MS-65. The overall eye appeal still must be positive or the coin does not merit the MS-65 grade.

MS-64

Marks: There may be numerous minor marks/hairlines, or other defects. There may be a few minor, or one or two significant, marks/hairlines in the main focal areas. On larger coins, these marks/hairlines may be more severe in the fields or main focal areas. However, a severe mark/hairline would have to be of a size that would preclude grading the coin MS-65, though not so severe as to reduce the coin to MS-63. If there are several fairly heavy marks/hairlines in obvious areas, the coin would grade MS-63 or lower.
Strike: The strike will range from average to full.
Luster: The luster can be slightly below average to full, and toning can impede the luster. On brilliant coins, there may be breaks in the luster caused by marks or hairlines.
Eye Appeal: The eye appeal can range from slightly negative to very positive. This is a nice coin, so anything too negative would preclude the MS-64 grade. Balance is a key. A coin with marks/hairlines in obvious focal areas would have to have great luster, or some other positive factor, to attain MS-64.

MS-63

Marks: There may be numerous marks/hairlines, including several major marks/hairlines in major focal areas. If there are distracting marks/hairlines on the major devices, the fields should be relatively clean. If there are distracting marks/hairlines in the fields, the devices should have less disurbance.
Strike: The strike will range from slightly below average to full.
Luster: The luster can be below average to full. The toning can seriously impede the luster. On brilliant coins, there can be significant breaks in the luster.
Eye Appeal: The eye appeal can be slightly negative to very positive. The "average" MS-63 will have neutral eye appeal (noticeable marks/hairlines, average to above average strike, average luster). However, quite a few coins are graded MS-63 because of their negative appearance. If either the luster, strike, or marks are below the standards set forth here, the one of the other criteria must be exceptional for the coin to attain MS-63.

MS-62

Marks: The marks/hairlines may cover most of the coin. If the marks are light, they may be scattered across the entire coin. If ther are several severe marks, the rest of the coin should be relatively clean.
Strike: The strike can range from very weak (some New Orleans Mint Morgan dollars) to full.
Luster: The luster can range from poor to vibrant.
Eye Appeal: The eye appeal can be negative to slightly positive. The negativity in this grade usually involves excessive marks or a poor strike or a lack of luster, or unattractive toning. Even coins that have overall positive eye appeal usually have one or two areas that are negative.

MS-61

Marks: There may be numerous marks across the entire coin, and several severe contact marks or hairlines. On larger coins there may be areas with almost no marks.
Strike: The strike will range from very weak to full.
Luster: The luster may be poor, average, or full.
Eye Appeal: The eye appeal will be negative to slightly positive.

MS-60

Marks: Numerous. Marks will cover most of the coin's surface. The marks can be large and in prime focal areas.
Strike: The strike will range from very weak to full.
Luster: The luster can be below poor, average, or full.
Eye Appeal: The eye appeal can be very negative to neutral.
